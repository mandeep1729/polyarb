## Vision

### 10x Check
Instead of just grouping markets for display, build a **Market Consensus Engine** where groups are the primary analytical unit. Each group shows a consensus probability, disagreement score, historical consensus chart, and best-odds routing. Users see "Tesla Q1 Deliveries: 67% consensus, 9pt Kalshi-vs-Polymarket spread, trending +8pts this week" — not 70k individual market rows.

### Platonic Ideal
The user opens polyarb and sees a feed of market consensus cards — not individual markets, not pairs, but topics the world is betting on. Each card shows the question, consensus probability, disagreement score, a 7-day sparkline, and expands to show every individual market sorted by best odds. The feeling: "I understand what the market thinks about X in 3 seconds."

## Key Implementation Decisions
- **Group membership:** Many-to-many (market_group_members join table)
- **Cross-platform merge threshold:** Configurable via env var, default 0.70
- **Homepage:** Group feed replaces current market listing as default

## Accepted Scope (added to this plan)
- Liquidity-weighted consensus probability per group
- Disagreement score (max spread) + best-odds indicator per group
- Group-level historical consensus chart (aggregated from price_snapshots)
- Group consensus cards as primary browse experience (frontend)
- Polymarket slug/group capture during market sync
- Cross-platform group merging via TF-IDF comparison of group canonical questions
- Group-level price alerts (consensus change, new spread detection)
